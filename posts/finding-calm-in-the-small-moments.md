Over the past few weeks, I’ve been talking to many clients about the pressure to “feel better quickly.” We often imagine healing as a dramatic shift — a breakthrough moment where everything suddenly makes sense. But in reality, healing usually happens in the small, quiet moments we barely notice.

It might be the first time you pause before reacting. The first time you speak to yourself with a little more kindness. The first time you realise you’re breathing more deeply than usual.

These moments don’t feel big, but they *are* big. They’re signs that your nervous system is slowly learning safety again. They’re signs that you’re building a different relationship with yourself — one that’s softer, steadier, and more compassionate.

If you’re in a season where everything feels heavy or slow, try this:

* Notice one small moment of calm each day
* Name it, even if it feels insignificant
* Let yourself acknowledge that this, too, is progress

Healing isn’t loud. It’s gentle, patient, and often quiet. And you’re doing better than you think.
